Section 5: Creating New Content Sections

Now that you know how styling works, it’s time to create your own section. This is where StackPilot truly becomes your site.

Where Content Lives

All content sections go in the /content folder. Each file should be named like sectionX-content.html where X is the section number or name. 

content/
├── index-content.html
├── section2-content.html
├── section3-content.html
└── section5-content.html  ← you’re here!

How It Loads

When the user navigates to #section5 in the URL, JavaScript fetches section5-content.html and injects it into the layout's <main> tag.

Writing Your Section

Inside your content file, don’t include full HTML. Just include what belongs inside the <main> area — headings, paragraphs, lists, etc. You can style with Tailwind (or the system you chose). 

<h1 class="text-3xl font-bold mb-4">Section Title</h1>
<p>This is your custom section content. Add tips, guides, whatever you need.</p>

Testing Your Content

Open index.html in your browser and add #section5 to the URL. If your file is named correctly and in the right place, it’ll load automatically.

Pro Tip: You can create any number of new sections this way. Just follow the naming rules and StackPilot will handle the rest.
